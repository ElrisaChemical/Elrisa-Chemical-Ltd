Competitor Insight

Why cashewderigh.com ranks above you — and what to copy

Cashew Derivation Ghana Ltd cashewderigh.com

A licensed Free Zones Company in Accra with a factory near Tema Port. They process cashew shells into technical CNSL and pelletised biomass fuel. Their website is simple — yet they dominate CNSL search terms. Here is exactly why, and what Elrisa can do about each factor.

Factor 01 · Topical Authority
Their entire site is about CNSL. Every page reinforces one topic.
→ Elrisa spreads across CNSL + polyols + phenolic resins + contract processing. That is not a weakness — but each topic needs its own tight cluster of pages. Start with CNSL since it drives 30%+ of all tracked queries.
Factor 02 · Physical Location Signals
"Factory near Tema Port, Accra" — explicit geographic anchor.
→ Elrisa has no physical address to publish. Compensate with explicit country/region language on every page: "Ghana-sourced", "West Africa", "exported to USA and Europe". Google uses location language as a proxy signal.
Factor 03 · E-E-A-T: Team & People
They have a Team page. Showing humans behind the company is a direct Google E-E-A-T signal (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).
→ Elrisa needs a Team or Leadership section. Even two or three brief profiles with relevant backgrounds (chemical engineering, agriculture, trade) will materially improve how Google evaluates the site's authority.
Factor 04 · Laboratory & Quality Signals
They mention a "24/7 laboratory" that tests throughout the production chain. This is a credibility signal even if not proven to the reader.
→ Elrisa should surface quality language explicitly: testing protocols, quality standards we adhere to (ISO, ASTM if applicable), and the processes we use to ensure consistency. This does not require a TDS — it requires language.
Factor 05 · Corporate Legitimacy
They publish company registration details: "Incorporated June 9, 2021 under Ghana's Companies Act 2019". Official credentials build trust with both users and Google.
→ Elrisa should add registration details (company number, regulatory body, year established) to the About page and schema markup. This information costs nothing to add and adds meaningful trust signals.
Factor 06 · What Cashewderigh Is NOT Doing
They do not target "phenol-free" positioning. They do not have US/North America export content. They do not cover downstream products (polyols, resins).
→ These are Elrisa's competitive advantages. The "phenol-free" angle on your phenolic resins is a direct gap cashewderigh cannot fill. This is where Elrisa wins.

Reference

Google SEO Starter Guide — rules that apply directly to this site

To Do
Rule: Title tags must be unique per page and accurately describe that page only
Google's guide states: "a good title is unique to the page, clear and concise, and accurately describes the contents." Having both cnsl.html and phenolic-resins.html mention "bio-based" and "Ghana" without differentiation makes Google unsure which page to rank for which query. Each page title must name its specific product and its specific benefit — not a generic brand tagline.
To Do
Rule: Write naturally for the buyer, not for the search engine
Google explicitly warns against keyword stuffing. The polyols.html keywords meta tag currently contains 35 separate keyword phrases — Google ignores this tag entirely. Remove it or reduce it. Instead, use the target keywords naturally once or twice in the page headings and body copy. Write the way a buyer talks: "phenol-free phenolic resin for brake pads" is a sentence a buyer writes. Use that exact phrase in a heading.
To Do
Rule: Use descriptive alt text on all product images
Google's guide specifically calls out image alt text as both an accessibility requirement and a search signal. Alt text should describe the image in relation to the content, not just the file name. Example: instead of alt="CNS.jpeg" use alt="Cashew nut shells being processed for CNSL extraction in Ghana". This also enters Elrisa into Google Images results — a search channel being entirely missed currently.
To Do
Rule: Structured data enables enhanced search result features
Google's guide states structured data (schema.org) enables "special features in search results including review stars, carousels, and more." The FAQ schema recommended in Phase 2 is the most accessible of these for Elrisa. Once FAQ schema is live on the CNSL page, Google may display an expanded FAQ directly in search results — this makes the listing taller, more visible, and significantly increases CTR without any rank change.
To Do
Rule: Content should demonstrate expertise — not just describe it
Google rewards pages that show expertise through specific, accurate detail. A page that says "CNSL is used in many industrial applications" is generic. A page that says "CNSL's naturally long C15 alkyl side chain gives it thermal stability above 200°C — making it the preferred bio-based binder in friction formulations" demonstrates knowledge. That specificity ranks. Write product content at the level a buyer who understands the chemistry would find useful — not at the level of a general overview.
